Facts About Cameroon

  • Cameroon is often referred to as "Africa in miniature" because of its diverse geography and culture.
  • The country has over 250 local languages and dialects, along with French and English as its official languages.
  • Mount Cameroon, located near the Gulf of Guinea, is the highest peak in Central and West Africa.
  • Cameroon has a rich wildlife with several national parks, such as Waza National Park and Korup National Park.
  • Cameroon is home to the second largest rainforest in the Congo Basin, after the Democratic Republic of Congo.
  • The country gained independence from France in 1960 and from Britain in 1961, leading to the formation of the Republic of Cameroon.
